# Privacy Policy for NSFW Shield

**Last Updated: March 6, 2026**

NSFW Shield ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. Unlike traditional filtering apps, NSFW Shield is built on a **Local-First Architecture**. This means your private browsing data, screen content, and habits never leave your device.

### 1. Data Collection & Usage
We do not collect, store, or transmit any sensitive user data to external servers.

*   **Screen Monitoring (Accessibility Service):** Our app uses the Android Accessibility Service to detect explicit text in real-time. All analysis is performed **locally on your device**. We do not take screenshots, record your screen, or upload any screen content.
*   **Web Filtering (VPN Service):** We use a local VPN interface to provide DNS-level filtering. This allows us to block explicit domains. **Your internet traffic is not routed through any external proxy or server.** All filtering decisions happen on-device.
*   **On-Device AI:** Content detection is powered by local machine learning models (TensorFlow Lite). No data is sent to the cloud for analysis.

### 2. Accountability Partner Reports
If you enable the Accountability Partner feature, the app will generate periodic reports.
*   **Metadata Only:** These reports contain only aggregated statistics (e.g., "50 sites blocked," "Main category: Adult Content"). 
*   **No URLs or Content:** Reports **never** contain specific URLs, search queries, or private content.
*   **Transmission:** Reports are sent via an encrypted connection to our automated email delivery system to be forwarded to your chosen partner.

### 3. Permissions
*   **Device Administrator:** Used exclusively for "Uninstall Protection" to prevent the app from being removed without your Admin PIN.
*   **Accessibility Service:** Used to detect and blur explicit content within third-party apps.
*   **VPN Service:** Used for system-wide DNS filtering.

### 4. Data Security
Because we do not store your data on our servers, there is no central database to breach. Your settings and app logs are stored in an encrypted private database on your physical device.
